\section*{Acknowledgements}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Acknowledgements}

My deepest thanks go to Professor Gustavo Didier, whose guidance shaped every part of this study: our weekly meetings set the direction and sharpened the arguments, and his careful comments on each draft taught me as much about writing mathematics as about the mathematics itself. The readings he assembled became the report's backbone, spanning its every part: Casella and Berger and Strang beneath the foundations; the monograph of Yao, Zheng, and Bai above all; Johnstone's spiked model and the phase transition of Baik, Ben Arous, and P\'ech\'e; the eigenvector theorems of Paul and of F\'eral and P\'ech\'e; and the spectral clustering of Lebeau, Chatelain, and Couillet, alongside the wider collection of texts and papers gathered in the course's resource folder. Joseph Genzer's honors thesis \citep{Genzer2025} served as a guide throughout, in both its structure and its standard. I thank the Department of Mathematics at Tulane University for making this independent study possible.

Large language models assisted along the way: Claude Code \citep{ClaudeCode2026} as a drafting and programming assistant working under my direction, with ChatGPT \citep{ChatGPT2026} and Gemini \citep{Gemini2026} supporting my study of the material. Every mathematical statement produced with their help was checked against the cited literature.
