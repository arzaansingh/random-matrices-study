\subsection{Moments and the Moment Generating Function}\label{subsec:mgf}

\todo{Content to fill in (from v5 MGF section, condensed):
- Definition: \(M_X(t) = \E[e^{tX}]\), existence on \((-h, h)\).
- Proposition: \(M_X^{(n)}(0) = \E[X^n]\) (one-line proof, differentiate under
  the integral).
- One worked example: \(\Normal{\mu, \sigma^2}\) MGF \(= e^{\mu t + \sigma^2 t^2/2}\).
- Note that heavy-tailed laws (e.g., Cauchy) lack an MGF; defer
  characteristic functions to Appendix~\ref{app:characteristic-functions}.}
